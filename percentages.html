Percentages

By: Justin

To: Hoshi and anyone who wants to learn



Example: Learn to solve "25% of what number is 40?"

Step 1:

Convert words/phrases to math. "What" or "What number" --> Any variable (x, y, z, etc) (anything that has "what") "of" --> Multiplication (*) "is" --> Equals (=) Examples: "24% of 30 is what?" --> "24% * 30 = x" and solve for x. "What number is 30% of 50?" ---> "x = 30% * 50" and solve for x. "56% of what number is 30?" ---> "56% * x = 30" and solve for x.

Step 2:

Convert percentages to decimals: % = 0.01 Multiply by 0.01 or move the decimal place to the left two spots. (Dividing by 100 works too.) Examples: 14% = 14 * 0.01 = 0.14 24% = 24 * 0.01 = 0.24 96% = 96 * 0.01 = 0.96 47% = 47 * 0.01 = 0.47 63% = 63 * 0.01 = 0.63 More examples: 234% = 234 * 0.01 = 2.34 3% = 3 * 0.01 = 0.03 47.3% = 47.3 * 0.01 = 0.473

Step 3:

Solve for the variable: "25% of what number is 40?" 25% * x = 40 (Step 1: Converting to math language) 0.25 * x = 40 (Step 2: Converting percentages to decimals) 0.25x = 40 x = 160 (Step 3: Solve for the variable) Answer: 160 25% of 160 is indeed 40!
